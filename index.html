Arbor-CLIP: A Large Multi-Modality Dataset for Advancing AI in Agriculture

This repository comprises scripts and sample data for processing and training a foundational model called "ArborCLIP," which builds upon the BioCLIP and OpenCLIP models, and is specifically designed for agricultural-relevant images.

Abstract

In this paper, we introduce Arbor-CLIP, the largest and most comprehensive publicly accessible dataset designed for advancing artificial intelligence (AI) in agriculture. Arbor-CLIP comprises 80 million images, surpassing all existing datasets in scale and diversity. This collection includes species from Aves, Arachnida, Insecta, Plantae, Fungi, Mollusca, and Reptilia, making it a pivotal resource for text-image multi-modality AI applications in agriculture and biology research. Each image is annotated with scientific names, taxonomic details, and common names, ensuring detailed and accurate data for training robust AI models. Leveraging the power of CLIP-like models, we developed the Arbor-CLIP-36M-model using a subset contain ing 36 million data points, Arbor-CLIP-36M. To facilitate rigorous evaluation, we created a benchmark dataset with four subsets, including rare species (Arbor-CLIP- Rare) and different life stages of insects (Arbor-CLIP-life-stages). Our benchmark reports include accuracy metrics, one-shot and k-shot learning performance, and evaluations across different life stages. Detailed taxonomic information enhances the model's ability to generalize across various species, facilitating advancements in species recognition, ecological moni toring, and conservation efforts. The combination of common names and taxonomic hierarchy helps bridge the gap between embedding Latin tokens and English tokens in text embedding models. By providing a large and diverse dataset, Arbor-CLIP enables the development of AI models that can improve pest control strategies, optimize crop monitoring, and contribute to biodiversity assessment and environmental conservation. These fields are critical for ensuring food security, preserving ecosystems, and mitigating the impacts of climate change. The Arbor-CLIP dataset is ready for immediate use, easily accessible, and publicly available, providing a valuable resource for future research and development in AI and agriculture.
