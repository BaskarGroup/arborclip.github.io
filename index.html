Arboretum: A Large Multimodal Dataset for Advancing AI for Biodiversity

This repository comprises scripts and sample data for processing and training a foundational model called "ArborCLIP," which builds upon the BioCLIP and OpenCLIP models, and is specifically designed for agricultural-relevant images.

Abstract

In this paper, we introduce Arboretum, the largest and most comprehensive publicly accessible dataset designed to advance AI for biodiversity. Arboretum includes 126 million images, surpassing existing datasets in scale and diversity, and is user-friendly for researchers. This collection encompasses image-language paired data for a diverse set of species from birds (Aves), spiders/ticks/mites (Arachnida), insects (Insecta), plants (Plantae), fungus/mushrooms (Fungi), snails (Mollusca), and snakes/lizards (Reptilia), making it a valuable resource for text-image multimodal AI applications in biodiversity and conservation, natural resource and ecology management, and agriculture research. Each image is annotated with scientific names, taxonomic details, and common names, ensuring accurate data for robust AI model training. Detailed taxonomic information enhances the model's ability to generalize across various species, which is crucial for scientific use in species recognition, ecological monitoring, and conservation efforts. We developed the ArborClip-40M model using a subset of 40 million images and report benchmark datasets for rigorous evaluation, reporting accuracy for zero-shot and few-shot learning, and evaluations across life stages, rare species, confounding species, and various levels of the taxonomic hierarchy. By providing a large and diverse dataset, Arboretum enables the development of AI models that can enhance pest control strategies, optimize crop monitoring, and contribute to biodiversity assessment and environmental conservation. These advancements are critical for ensuring food security, preserving ecosystems, and mitigating the impacts of climate change. The Arboretum dataset is ready for immediate use, is easily accessible, and publicly available.
